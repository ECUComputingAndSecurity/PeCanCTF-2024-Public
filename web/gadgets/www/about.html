A little history about Inspector Gadget

Inspector Gadget is a beloved animated television series that originally aired in the 1980s. The show was created by DIC Entertainment, a French-American animation production company, and it became an iconic part of many childhoods around the world.

The concept for Inspector Gadget was developed by Andy Heyward, Jean Chalopin, and Bruno Bianchi. The idea was to create a humorous and action-packed detective show featuring a bumbling cyborg detective equipped with an array of gadgets. The character of Inspector Gadget was loosely inspired by Inspector Clouseau from the Pink Panther series, combining elements of slapstick comedy with a detective genre.

Inspector Gadget premiered on September 12, 1983. The series ran for two seasons, with a total of 86 episodes. Each episode followed Inspector Gadget as he tried to thwart the evil plans of Dr. Claw, the leader of the criminal organization M.A.D.
